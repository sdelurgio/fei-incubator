STARTER IDEA 1 · NUK & GRACO
Who is today's first-year parent —
and are NUK and Graco
actually talking to them?
The profile of the first-year parent has shifted significantly. They are more informed, more online, and more deliberate about the products and brands they choose. Research shows today's parents feel underserved by baby brands that speak to a narrow version of the parenting experience. NUK and Graco make products that help babies thrive — but the bigger opportunity may be in reflecting the full range of people doing the caring.
Got something? Even half a thought counts.
↗ Submit your idea
👐
The primary caregiver
The person doing daily pickups, night feeds, and solo weekends looks different than it did a generation ago. Is the Graco experience — from packaging to marketing to UX — designed for all of them?
🤝
The village
New parents are building their village online before the baby arrives — in Reddit threads, group chats, and parenting apps. What's the NUK product or moment that earns a place in that space?
🏷️
The brand gap
Today's parents are vocal about what brands get right — and what they miss. Where is the white space that NUK or Graco could genuinely own that no competitor is addressing well right now?
A few sparks to get you going
→ A Graco product or campaign designed to speak to the full spectrum of who is doing the caregiving — not just one version of a parent
→ A NUK product that reflects the way feeding actually happens in modern households
→ Something in packaging, messaging, or UX that signals: we see how parenting has changed
→ A content or community play that positions NUK as the brand that actually reflects the modern parenting experience
→ A partnership that bridges baby gear with postpartum support or the broader first-year experience
STARTER IDEA 2 · SHARPIE · PAPER MATE · ELMER'S · EXPO
CraftTok creators are making millions
of videos with our products.
They're just not talking about us.
#CraftTok, #ArtTok, and DIY communities on TikTok are among the most engaged audiences on the platform — and Sharpie markers, Elmer's glue, and EXPO boards show up in their content constantly. But these creators aren't tagging us, recommending us, or seeking us out. We're ambient. Niche art brands like Posca and Copic have genuine cult followings in these same communities. Why don't we?
Got something? Even half a thought counts.
↗ Submit your idea
🎬
The maker video
A CraftTok creator just filmed a satisfying art video using Sharpies. They didn't mention the brand once. What would it take — product, design, campaign — for them to hold it up and say "this is what I use"?
🖌️
The creator's toolkit
Art and craft creators are opinionated about their tools and vocal about their favorites. Posca and Copic have that loyalty. What would it take for a Sharpie or Paper Mate product to earn the same standing?
🛍️
The haul moment
Creator haul and "what's in my art bag" videos are high-converting TikTok formats. What's the L&D product that earns a spot in a CraftTok creator's haul — and gets recommended, not just used?
A few sparks to get you going
→ A Sharpie product line designed with the CraftTok creator in mind — something they'd hold up on camera and recommend by name
→ An Elmer's collab with a maker or DIY creator who has a genuine craft following, not a paid-media play
→ A "creator kit" bundle designed to be unboxed on camera — product selection, packaging, and all
→ Something that makes the craft process itself more visual or satisfying on video (ink flow, color payoff, a satisfying peel or reveal)
→ A brand presence in #CraftTok or #ArtTok that feels like a recommendation, not an ad — what would that actually look like?
STARTER IDEA 3 · WHOLE PORTFOLIO
The next big L&D product probably
starts in a niche corner of the internet.
What corner are we missing?
Stanley cups didn't go viral through a national campaign. They went viral because a specific community made them part of their identity — and then the whole world noticed. The same thing has happened to Posca pens, to duct tape craft, to Moleskine. Somewhere right now, a small but passionate community is using one of our products in a way we never intended. Or they're using a competitor's because we never showed up. What's the L&D play that starts niche and goes everywhere?
Got something? Even half a thought counts.
↗ Submit your idea
🔍
A community you've found
A subreddit, a TikTok hashtag, a Discord server where people are obsessed with something adjacent to our portfolio. What is it — and is there an L&D brand that belongs in that world?
👤
An identity we're ignoring
Who's already buying our products but never seeing themselves in our marketing? The adult crafter. The neurodivergent planner. The Gen Alpha bedroom decorator. What would it mean if we spoke directly to them?
⚡
The drop
What's the L&D limited release that sells out in 48 hours — not because of a paid campaign, but because the right community was waiting for it? What's the product, and who's the community?
A few sparks to get you going
→ A product or bundle built for a specific micro-community — gardeners, tattoo collectors, bullet journalists, high school theater kids
→ A limited drop that collaborates with a creator who already has a cult following in a space adjacent to our brands
→ Something that earns its way into a specific TikTok subculture — not through paid media, but because it actually belongs there
→ An Elmer's, Sharpie, or Paper Mate product that doesn't look like a school supply — and was never meant to
→ A product that exists entirely because of a micro-trend you've been watching — tell us what the trend is and who it's for
